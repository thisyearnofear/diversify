# Regional Financial Philosophies: Authentic Cultural Framings

## Overview

This document maps authentic financial philosophies from different regions to inform culturally-sensitive product design. These are not stereotypes but documented cultural values that shape how people think about wealth, community, and economic decisions.

**"Wealth that builds Africa"** - Africapitalism

### Key Concepts

**"Sumak Kawsay" (Buen Vivir)** - Good Living in harmony

- Collective well-being over GDP growth

- CELAC, ALBA, UNASUR integration
- Resistance to neoliberalism
- South-South cooperation
- Economic independence from Global North

**Economic Nationalism**:
- Protecting local industries
- Resource sovereignty
- Regional currency initiatives
- Anti-dollarization movements

### Financial Behaviors
- Regional diversification (LatAm-focused)
- Resistance to dollar dependence

## üåè Southeast Asia: Gotong Royong & Bayanihan

### Core Philosophy
**"Gotong Royong"** (Indonesian) - Mutual cooperation
**"Bayanihan"** (Filipino) - Being a hero to one another

### Key Concepts

**Gotong Royong**:
- Voluntary mutual assistance
- Community over individual
- Shared responsibility
- No expectation of monetary return
- Social cohesion through cooperation

**Bayanihan**:
- Collective action for common good
- Helping without expecting payment
- Community resilience
- Shared burden, shared benefit
- "Kapwa" (shared identity)

**Remittance Culture**:
- Supporting family back home
- Diaspora wealth building
- Cross-border family economics
- Overseas worker (OFW) contributions

### Financial Behaviors
- Remittances as primary financial activity
- Community lending (paluwagan, arisan)
- Family pooling for emergencies
- Informal mutual aid networks
- Collective investment in community projects

## üåç Middle East/North Africa: Islamic Finance & Zakat

### Core Philosophy
**"Wealth is a trust from Allah"**
**"Prohibition of riba (interest)"**

### Key Concepts

**Islamic Finance Principles**:
- No interest (riba) - profit-sharing instead
- No speculation (gharar)
- No haram investments (alcohol, gambling, pork)
- Asset-backed financing
- Risk-sharing over risk-transfer

**Zakat** (Obligatory charity):
- 2.5% of wealth annually to poor
- Wealth purification
- Social responsibility
- Redistribution mechanism

**Waqf** (Endowment):
- Perpetual charitable trust
- Community benefit
- Intergenerational giving

### Financial Behaviors
- Sharia-compliant investments only
- Gold as wealth preservation
- Family business structures
- Community lending (qard hassan)
- Avoiding conventional banking
